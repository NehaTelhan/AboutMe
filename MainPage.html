Externing at COS! What an experience!

During my Spring Recess in 2016, spent one week as an extern for a Charlottesville start-up called the Center for Open Science. Was part of the OSF (Open Science Framework) infrastructure team.

Worked one-to-one with mentor for a business week, shadowing office practices from daily Scrum meetings to catered lunches and presentation.

Experience using tools like Source Tree, JIRA, GitHub, and coding practices in python, html, and JavaScript.

The coolest part of this experience is having work I've done be visible on the live website. A bug-fix that involved evoking a pop-up window when navigating to another page without saving changes

Teaching Assistant in SEAS! "Teaching other students helped to reinforce my own understanding!"

Undergraduate teaching assistant for 'Introduction to Engineering' (ENGR 1620) with Professor Greg Lewin and for 'Science of Information: How the iPhone Works' (ECE 2066) with Professor Scott Acton.

Intro to ENGR

Introduction to Engineering involved working with students who went through the complete design process with an actual client, a local elementary school.

As a TA, I'd help groups figure out the logistics/implementations of their designs and possible problems they'd run into in either functionality or accessibility.

Teaching other students how to responsibly use tools, from tables saws to 3-D printers, was another important task.

Science of Information

Responsibilities for this class included weekly homework grading, holding office hours where students could ask questions from lecture and about the homework, and holding a lab section.

It was important for students to hear the information formally in lecture but then to see the concepts applied in the lab and homework.
